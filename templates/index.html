{# Government Incorporation Section #}

Incorporation with Government Initiatives

We are actively working towards incorporating LabourLink with relevant government schemes and initiatives aimed at supporting daily wage laborers. Our goal is to provide a recognized and reliable platform that aligns with government efforts to empower the workforce and streamline labor allocation in various sectors.

Aligned with Government Schemes

Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA)

LabourLink is a platform for connecting labor to various jobs, it could potentially complement MGNREGA by providing additional avenues for employment beyond the guaranteed days or for different types of work.

Skill India Mission

Schemes under this mission, like the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY), focus on skill development and certification. LabourLink can be a platform where skilled laborers (certified under such schemes) can find work matching their skills.

National Urban Livelihoods Mission (NULM)

While MGNREGA focuses on rural areas, NULM aims to reduce poverty and vulnerability of the urban poor households by enabling them to access gainful self-employment and skilled wage employment opportunities. Depending on whether your platform caters to urban laborers, NULM could be relevant.

Contributing to UN SDG Goals

SDG 1: No Poverty

By connecting laborers to consistent work opportunities, LabourLink helps reduce poverty.

SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

We promote inclusive and sustainable economic growth by facilitating decent work opportunities.

SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

LabourLink helps reduce inequality by providing equal access to job opportunities for laborers.
